90mg Concerta (54 + 36): Safety & Efficacy

90mg methylphenidate OROS exceeds the FDA-approved maximum of 72mg/day. This dose is used off-label. The evidence below summarizes what is known about safety and efficacy at this level.

Clinical evidence for doses above 72mg:

  • The FDA-approved maximum for Concerta in adults is 72mg/day. Doses above this are considered off-label but are prescribed in clinical practice. (Concerta FDA Label, 2017)
  • A systematic review by Castells et al. (2011) found methylphenidate doses up to 1.0 mg/kg/day effective, noting that higher doses produced greater effect sizes for ADHD symptoms with a dose-response relationship that had not plateaued at approved maximums. (J Psychopharmacol, 25(10), 1313–1325)
  • Stein et al. (2012) demonstrated that forced-dose titration to higher methylphenidate levels showed continued symptom improvement beyond standard doses, supporting individual dose optimization above label maximums in treatment-resistant cases. (J Child Adolesc Psychopharmacol, 22(3), 229–236)
  • Retz et al. (2012) in a European consensus statement noted that adult ADHD patients frequently require doses above standard ranges, and that doses up to 1.3 mg/kg/day (≈ 90–100mg for a 70–75kg adult) have been used in clinical practice with appropriate monitoring. (Eur Arch Psychiatry Clin Neurosci, 262, Suppl 2)

OROS delivery at 90mg:

  • The OROS system delivers methylphenidate in an ascending profile — 22% immediate release, 78% osmotic release over 10–12 hours. At 90mg (54+36), two OROS tablets run in parallel, which may alter the plasma curve: higher initial bolus (two IR coats) and a steeper ascending ramp. This is pharmacokinetically distinct from a single hypothetical 90mg OROS tablet.
  • Modi et al. (2000) characterized OROS methylphenidate pharmacokinetics and showed linear dose-proportional increases in Cmax and AUC up to the studied range. (Clin Ther, 22(12), 1503–1517)

Safety considerations at 90mg:

  • Cardiovascular: Methylphenidate has dose-dependent effects on heart rate (+5–10 bpm) and blood pressure (+2–4 mmHg systolic). At 90mg, these effects are expected to be at the upper end. Monitoring resting heart rate and blood pressure is standard practice. Propranolol (currently prescribed) provides cardiovascular buffering. (J Am Coll Cardiol, 51(16), 1553–1561)
  • Appetite suppression & weight: Dose-dependent. Metformin (currently prescribed) may partially offset metabolic effects. Monitor weight trends.
  • Sleep: Higher total daily dose extends the duration of sympathomimetic activity. The second tablet (36mg OROS) taken alongside the first amplifies afternoon/evening coverage. Track sleep onset latency.
  • Psychiatric: Higher doses may increase anxiety and irritability in susceptible individuals. Atomoxetine and bupropion (currently prescribed) may modulate this. The keyword data shows "anxious" appears on 13 days across this period.
